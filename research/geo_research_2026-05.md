## 2. Citation mechanics — what each AI engine actually pulls from

- **Reddit is the #1 source across every major AI engine, cited at roughly 40% frequency across LLMs.** Wikipedia is second, dominant on ChatGPT (26–48% of top-10 citation share). — source: https://www.tryprofound.com/blog/ai-platform-citation-patterns
- 5W's "AI Platform Citation Source Index 2026" identified 50 websites that disproportionately decide brand visibility inside ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini and Google AI Overviews — the top 15 domains capture ~68% of all consolidated AI citation share. — source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/5w-releases-ai-platform-citation-source-index-2026-the-50-websites-that-now-decide-what-brands-are-visible-inside-chatgpt-claude-perplexity-gemini-and-google-ai-overviews-302759804.html
- Citation volume per response: ChatGPT ~10.42 links per answer, Google AI Overviews ~9.26, Perplexity ~5.01. Perplexity ties every claim to a source 78% of the time vs. ChatGPT's 62%. — source: https://www.tryprofound.com/blog/ai-platform-citation-patterns
- Platform retrieval logic, summarised from Yext's analysis: Gemini is grounded in Google's index and behaves most like classic search; Perplexity pulls from a narrower mix of official sites and directories; Claude leans more on reviews and user-validated content. — source: https://www.yext.com/blog/how-chatgpt-perplexity-gemini-claude-decide-what-to-cite
- ChatGPT-specific: owned corporate-newsroom content drives ~18% of citations on ChatGPT vs. only ~3% on Google's AI surfaces — i.e. your own newsroom matters far more in ChatGPT than in AI Overviews. — source: https://signalgenesys.com/how-press-release-distribution-drives-llm-citations-signal-genesys-study/
- Google AI Overviews social-citation breakdown (Tinuiti Q1 2026): Reddit 21%, YouTube 18.8% — heavy preference for user-generated content even inside Google's own answer engine. — source: https://www.adweek.com/media/youtube-reddit-ai-search-engine-citations/
- A 2025 study of 15,847 AI Overview results: pages with 15+ recognised entities had 4.8x higher citation odds — entity density is now a measurable AI-citation lever. — source: https://www.metricsrule.com/research/entity-authority-gap-ai-trust-competitor/

## 3. What ranks well in AI answers — Reddit, news, reviews, schema

- Reddit's absolute citation volume keeps growing — Superlines tracking 62 brands over 30 days ending Feb 2026 logged 39,551 Reddit citations vs 15,735 YouTube. But Reddit's *share* of social citations is being compressed by surging YouTube and X. — source: https://www.superlines.io/articles/ai-search-statistics/
- YouTube has now overtaken Reddit as the #1 social citation source on AI search per Adweek/Goodie data — meaning video transcripts are an under-priced GEO asset. — source: https://www.adweek.com/media/youtube-reddit-ai-search-engine-citations/
- Comparative listicles ("best of", "top X for Y") drive **32.5% of AI citations across platforms** — the single highest-converting content format in the public dataset. — source: https://digitalagencynetwork.com/generative-engine-optimization-case-studies-real-examples-proven-strategies/
- Trustpilot became the **5th most-cited page on the internet by ChatGPT** in January 2026 — a single review platform that punches well above its weight in AI answers. — source: https://cmotech.uk/story/trustpilot-launches-ai-search-tools-for-brand-visibility
- Pages with 3–4 complementary schema types (Article + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList) get cited ~2x more often than pages with a single schema type. — source: https://wpriders.com/schema-markup-for-ai-search-types-that-get-you-cited/
- Schema markup correctly implemented can boost AI-citation probability by up to ~36%, per the WPRiders/SchemaApp synthesis of 2026 case data. — source: https://www.schemaapp.com/schema-markup/how-entity-seo-supports-brand-authority-in-ai-search/
- 74.2% of all AI citations come from structured "Top N" content; brands seeing the biggest lifts deploy "triple JSON-LD stacking" (Article + ItemList + FAQPage) on every ranking page. — source: https://digitalagencynetwork.com/generative-engine-optimization-case-studies-real-examples-proven-strategies/
- Press releases distributed via newswire pickup graph contribute ~0.04% of all AI citations (i.e. wire syndication on its own is essentially noise); direct citations from PR Newswire-class domains are ~0.21%. — source: https://authoritytech.io/curated/wire-distribution-ai-citations-press-release-2026
- Conversely: PR-style content has grown 5x in 2025 *as a share of citations*, but the growth is concentrated in coverage that gets republished on outlets AI already trusts (Yahoo Finance /news/, etc.), not on the wire itself. — source: https://superprompt.com/blog/press-releases-5x-more-ai-citations-new-pr-playbook-2026
- The Fullintel × University of Connecticut paper at IPRRC Feb 2026: **89%+ of links cited by AI were earned, unpaid coverage**. Earned media beats paid distribution by an order of magnitude. — source: https://authoritytech.io/blog/machine-relations-evidence-earned-media-ai-citations

## 7. Failure modes and skepticism — the legitimate critiques of GEO

- **Hallucination still meaningfully distorts citation outputs.** ChatGPT historically delivered factually wrong outputs in roughly one-third of responses; GPT-5 brought that to ~1.4% — an order-of-magnitude improvement, but not zero, and competitor models are uneven. — source: https://generative-engine.org/the-geo-tool-accuracy-crisis-what-brand-tracking-platforms-a-1760483035830
- **GEO measurement tools are unreliable.** A widely-cited 2026 piece called this "the GEO tool accuracy crisis": temporal variability, model fragmentation, opaque sampling. Counting a brand mention is "a blunt instrument" — a citation listing you as a top vendor is not the same as one listing you as a company with a past breach. — source: https://generative-engine.org/the-geo-tool-accuracy-crisis-what-brand-tracking-platforms-a-1760483035830
- **The acronym soup itself is a critique vector.** Stuart Bruce ("the PR Futurist"): "GEO/AEO/GAIO… really means influencing answers given by AI. Sometimes it's snake oil." Tyler Einberger compares the current GEO pitch wave to "anti snake oil SEO pitches from the 2010s." — source: https://www.stuartbruce.biz/2025/11/is-geo-essential-for-pr-or-is-it-all-snake-oil/
- **Mass AI content is a known failure mode.** Multiple 2026 critiques single out AI-spun articles, hidden keyword stuffing, and cloaking as "waste of money" tactics — they don't get cited and increasingly get penalised. — source: https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/answer-engine-optimization-trends
- **Wire press release distribution alone is mostly snake oil.** Wire-only distribution drives ~0.04% of AI citations — i.e. selling clients "PR distribution for AI visibility" without securing real earned coverage is a known gap between vendor pitch and outcome. — source: https://authoritytech.io/curated/wire-distribution-ai-citations-press-release-2026
- **Traditional SEO ranking does not directly buy you AI visibility** — but the relationship is asymmetric. Ahrefs found ~76% of AI Overview citations come from top-10 ranking pages; conversely, ChatGPT citations dropped 27.8% for sites that lost organic visibility. So SEO is necessary-but-not-sufficient, and pitching GEO as a *replacement* for SEO is wrong. — source: https://www.conductor.com/academy/state-of-aeo-geo-report/
- **GEO isn't a quick fix.** Across published case studies, visibility shifts before traffic does — and sustained results require continuous optimisation, not a one-off audit. New content enters AI citation pools within 3–5 business days, but durable wins compound over months. — source: https://digitalagencynetwork.com/generative-engine-optimization-case-studies-real-examples-proven-strategies/
- **The Rand Fishkin/iPullRank line** is more nuanced: they argue against the acronym-chase but agree the underlying mechanics (entity authority, multi-platform earned mentions, structured data) are real and will dominate. The honest framing is "this is just SEO under a different surface" rather than "this is a brand-new discipline you must buy from us." — source: https://www.siegemedia.com/conversation/seo-vs-geo-vs-aeo-which-one-is-it-w-rand-fishkin

## 8. Concrete tactics that move the needle — ranked by published evidence

Ranked from strongest to weakest published evidence base.

1. **Get on third-party "best of" listicles in your category.** 32.5% of AI citations across platforms come from comparative listicles; brands mentioned on 4+ platforms are 2.8x more likely to appear in ChatGPT responses; 85% of AI brand mentions come from third-party pages. — source: https://digitalagencynetwork.com/generative-engine-optimization-case-studies-real-examples-proven-strategies/ and https://alhena.ai/blog/geo-citation-strategy-off-site-sources-ai-recommendations/
2. **Build entity authority — not just backlinks.** Pages with 15+ recognised entities had 4.8x higher AI citation odds. Use sameAs to link your business to Wikipedia, Wikidata, LinkedIn, Google Knowledge Graph. Establish the relationships, not just the mentions. — source: https://www.metricsrule.com/research/entity-authority-gap-ai-trust-competitor/
3. **Stack schema: LocalBusiness + FAQPage + Service + BreadcrumbList.** 3–4 complementary schema types deliver ~2x citation rate vs. one. FAQ schema alone makes pages 3x more likely to be featured in AI Overviews. — source: https://wpriders.com/schema-markup-for-ai-search-types-that-get-you-cited/
4. **Earned editorial coverage on already-trusted outlets.** 89%+ of AI-cited links are earned/unpaid (Fullintel × UConn). Targeting one analytical piece in an industry-respected publication beats 500 wire syndications. — source: https://authoritytech.io/blog/machine-relations-evidence-earned-media-ai-citations
5. **Reddit + Trustpilot + Google review presence — multi-platform, not single.** Reddit is the #1 cited domain across all major LLMs; Trustpilot is now ChatGPT's 5th most-cited page. Brands with claims appearing across 5+ external domains see citation rates improve by ~67%. — source: https://wellows.com/blog/social-media-ai-citations-report-2026/ and https://cmotech.uk/story/trustpilot-launches-ai-search-tools-for-brand-visibility
6. **Podcast guesting as an entity-credibility signal.** Each appearance generates show-note backlinks plus entity associations — AI sees the podcast vouching for you and transfers some of its authority. — source: https://amplify.matchmaker.fm/how-to-use-podcast-guesting-for-aeo-2026/
7. **YouTube content + transcripts.** YouTube has overtaken Reddit as the top social citation source; transcripts give AI an extractable text version of the same content — under-priced in current trade-SMB GEO. — source: https://pikaseo.com/articles/youtube-overtakes-reddit-ai-citations
8. **Bold, quotable, quantitative claims in content.** Quantitative claims earn ~40% higher citation rates than qualitative ones; AI prefers extractable, numbered, framework-style answers. — source: https://www.demandlocal.com/blog/geo-strategies-agencies-cited-ai-answers/
9. **Active Google Business Profile maintenance.** GBP feeds AI Overviews directly for local queries; profiles that go 30+ days without photos/posts now show measurable visibility drops post-March 2026 update. — source: https://www.marketingcode.com/google-march-core-update-gbp-ai-search-contractors-2026/
10. **Press release distribution — only as part of an earned-coverage funnel.** Pure wire = 0.04% of citations. Newsroom-on-trusted-outlet (Yahoo Finance /news/ etc.) = confirmed ChatGPT citation paths. The PR play is real but is not "press release as AI hack." — source: https://signalgenesys.com/how-press-release-distribution-drives-llm-citations-signal-genesys-study/

## Synthesis: Five things this changes about FastGEO's strategy

**1. FastGEO's price point is its sharpest weapon, but only if positioned correctly.**
The market floor for an "SME GEO" retainer is genuinely £1,500/month (https://www.margen.net/best-geo-agency-for-smes-uk-2026/) and most listed UK GEO agencies (Hallam, Rise at Seven, Passion, Blue Array) target mid-market+ with no published pricing. A £150 audit and a £997/mo cap is not a discount on this market — it is a different market. Position FastGEO as the *only* GEO offering trades and local SMEs can buy without having to "Contact Sales." Lead with that asymmetry rather than competing on capability lists.

**2. Sell entity-authority and multi-platform mentions, not press releases.**
The strongest evidence base in 2026 is around third-party listicle placement, earned editorial coverage, schema-stacking, and multi-platform reviews. Wire-only PR drives ~0.04% of AI citations; "PR distribution for AI" is the snake-oil edge of the market. FastGEO's productised offer should be a "GEO Foundation" — schema stack + Trustpilot/Checkatrade/Reddit profile + 3 listicle placements + Wikidata/Knowledge Graph claim — not a press-release retainer. That keeps the operation deliverable inside £997/mo and aligns with the strongest 2026 evidence.

**3. Lead with the local-intent gap, not the AI-revolution narrative.**
The Orbit Media data shows Google still beats AI Chat for *local* queries (24% prefer AI for local, vs. higher for research) — but AI's local share is rising fast (consumer use of ChatGPT for local-business research went from 6% → 45% YoY). The most defensible content thesis is not "AI is replacing Google" — it is "your customers are *also* using AI now, and you are invisible there." This survives skeptical readers (no overclaim) and still lands the buying argument.

**4. Productise around a trade-and-postcode "AI Visibility Audit" — and make the deliverable a competitor comparison.**
The strongest case-study format in 2026 is comparative listicles and third-party validation. FastGEO's £150 audit should output: (a) actual ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Overviews queries for the trader's category in their postcode, (b) which local competitors got named, (c) the schema/entity/review delta between the trader and the named competitors, (d) a 5-action fix list. This mirrors how AI itself thinks — comparatively — and gives the prospect an emotional reason to upgrade to the £997 retainer.

**5. Bake Reddit/Checkatrade/Trustpilot/YouTube into the retainer — they are the channels AI actually reads.**
For UK trades the highest-leverage off-page assets are Checkatrade, Trustpilot, Reddit (r/UKtradespeople, r/DIYUK, r/HousingUK), and YouTube. Reddit is the #1 LLM citation source globally; Trustpilot is ChatGPT's 5th most-cited page; Checkatrade dominates UK trade-query SERPs. Wrap a productised "Mention Stack" service into the retainer (review-flow setup, Reddit participation playbook, 1 short YouTube/transcript per month, listicle pitch outreach). This is cheap to deliver, durable, and is exactly what the 2026 citation data says works — instead of producing yet more on-site AI content nobody reads.
