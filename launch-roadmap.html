Why FastGEO Wins

Most UK GEO agencies target enterprise clients at premium prices. Tradespeople, local services, and small businesses are completely underserved. They don't need a £5,000/month retainer — they need someone to sort their AI visibility for a fair price and explain it in plain English.

FastGEO's cost base is extremely lean. Press releases via EIN Presswire cost roughly £53 each at bulk rates. Selling a single full authority press release at £397 means healthy margins from day one, with no staff overhead.

10 Ways to Get the Message Out

Ranked by speed to revenue. Each method includes what to do, when to start, and what it costs.

1

Cold Email Outreach

Week 1 £0 cost

You already have 94 verified emails from the StakScan prospect list. These are real UK service businesses. Write a 3 email sequence: first email shows them what happens when you ask ChatGPT to recommend their trade in their area (spoiler: they're not there), second email explains why, third offers the £150 GEO Starter. Keep it short, personal, and outcome focused.

Target: 5–10 replies from 94 emails. Even 2 conversions = £300+ revenue and proof of concept.

2

SEO Blog Content

Week 1–2 £0 cost

People are already searching "how to get my business on ChatGPT", "how to appear in AI search", and "generative engine optimization UK". Write 5 blog posts targeting these queries. Each post should be genuinely helpful, answer the question properly, and end with a soft CTA to the GEO Starter. This is the long game — it compounds over months. Use the blog writing style: short paragraphs, lots of white space, conversational tone.

Priority blog topics:

"How to Get Your Business Recommended by ChatGPT" · "What Is Generative Engine Optimization (and Why Should You Care?)" · "Why Your Business Doesn't Show Up on AI Search Tools" · "GEO vs SEO: What's the Difference and Do You Need Both?" · "The Small Business Owner's Guide to AI Visibility"

3

FastGEO's Own Press Release

Week 1 ~£53

Eat your own cooking. Write and distribute a press release announcing FastGEO as the UK's first affordable GEO agency. This does three things at once: it gets FastGEO cited across 100+ news sites (which helps your own AI visibility), it gives you hands on experience with the exact process you're selling, and it creates a case study you can reference with clients ("here's what we did for ourselves").

Angle: "UK agency launches to help small businesses get found by ChatGPT and Claude" — newsworthy because GEO is genuinely new and the price point is disruptive.

4

LinkedIn Content

Week 1 £0 cost

Post 3 times per week showing real examples of AI search results. The format that works: screenshot of ChatGPT recommending (or not recommending) a local business, then a short explanation of what's happening and what to do about it. People love seeing their own industry mentioned. Tag specific trades and locations. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards native content and conversation starters.

Content ideas: "I asked ChatGPT to recommend a plumber in Leeds — here's what happened" · Before/after GEO results · Quick tips business owners can try right now.

5

Facebook Groups (Trade Specific)

Week 2 £0 cost

Join 10–15 Facebook groups for UK tradespeople (plumbers, electricians, builders, landscapers). Don't sell immediately — post genuinely useful content first. Show them how to check if AI tools recommend them. Once you've built some credibility, share your GEO Starter offer. These groups are goldmines because the members are exactly your target market and they actively discuss marketing.

Groups to find: "UK Plumbers", "Electricians UK", "Builders Talk Group", "UK Tradesmen and Women", local business networking groups.

6

Free GEO Check Lead Magnet

Week 2–3 £0 cost

Create a simple page where business owners enter their trade and location. You manually check ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity for them and email back a short report showing whether they appear. This is your version of the StakScan free scan — it captures their email, demonstrates the problem, and naturally leads to "want us to fix this?" You could even reuse StakScan's Zapier/Make workflow for the booking funnel.

Conversion path: Free check → email with results → "Book a call to discuss your GEO Starter" → sale.

7

YouTube Explainer Videos

Week 3–4 £0 cost

Screen recordings showing real AI search results. "I asked ChatGPT to find a plumber in Manchester — watch what happens." These are incredibly easy to make (just screen record + talk over it) and they rank well on YouTube for "how to get on ChatGPT" type queries. Each video should be 3–5 minutes, end with a CTA to the free GEO check or directly to fastgeo.co.uk.

Why this works: People search YouTube for "how to" questions. These videos also get embedded in AI training data, helping FastGEO's own visibility.

8

Referral Programme

Week 4 £0 upfront

Once you have your first few clients, offer them £25 off their next month (or a flat £25 Amazon voucher) for every business they refer who signs up. Tradespeople talk to each other constantly — at suppliers, on jobs, in WhatsApp groups. A happy client telling their mate "this bloke got me showing up on ChatGPT for £150" is worth more than any ad.

Keep it simple: No affiliate links or tracking codes. Just "tell them to mention your name when they book."

9

Local Business Networking Events

Week 4–6 £10–30/event

Attend BNI chapters, local chamber of commerce events, and trade breakfasts. Your pitch is a live demo: pull out your phone, open ChatGPT, ask it to recommend a business like theirs in their area. When they're not there, hand them your card. This is devastatingly effective in person because the reaction is always the same — genuine surprise followed by "how do I fix that?"

Prep: Have 5–6 pre researched examples for common trades in the local area ready to show.

10

Paid Ads (Google + Facebook)

Week 6–8 £200–500/mo

Only after you've validated the offer with organic methods. Target Google searches like "how to get on ChatGPT", "AI search optimization UK", "generative engine optimization". Facebook ads targeting UK business owners, particularly trades. Start with £10/day on Google, £10/day on Facebook, test for 2 weeks, cut what doesn't work, double what does.

Why last: Paid ads amplify what already works. If you can't sell GEO organically, ads won't fix it. Get the message right first, then pour fuel on it.

8 Week Launch Timeline

Week 1 starts the day you decide to go. Everything is sequenced so earlier work feeds later work.

Week 1 — Launch Foundation

Foundation

Set up Stripe products (GEO Starter £150, Kickstart £397, Monthly £497/mo, Premium £997/mo) in the existing StakScan Stripe account — just create new products and payment links, no new account needed. Send the first cold email sequence to the 94 verified prospects. Write and distribute FastGEO's own press release via EIN Presswire. Start daily LinkedIn posting.

Week 2 — Content Engine

Content

Publish first 2 blog posts (target: "how to get business on ChatGPT" and "what is GEO"). Join 10+ Facebook trade groups and start posting helpful content. Send follow up emails to non responders from week 1. Set up the free GEO check landing page.

Week 3 — Expand Reach

Growth

Publish blog posts 3 and 4. Record first YouTube video (screen recording of AI search demo). Start promoting the free GEO check on LinkedIn and Facebook groups. Cold email batch 2 — find another 50–100 prospects using the same process as StakScan.

Week 4 — Referrals & Networking

Growth

By now you should have your first paying clients. Launch the referral programme. Attend your first networking event with the live demo pitch. Publish blog post 5. Second YouTube video. Assess what's working — double down on the channels generating leads.

Weeks 5–6 — Optimise & Scale

Scale

Review results from all channels. Cut anything with zero traction. Publish case studies from early clients (with permission). Start testing small paid ad budgets on Google and Facebook. Continue weekly blog posts and LinkedIn content. Build a second cold email list of 200+ prospects.

Weeks 7–8 — Revenue Machine

Revenue

By week 8 you should have a repeatable process: you know which channels work, you have testimonials and case studies, and you're converting leads consistently. Focus on upselling GEO Starter clients to Monthly packages. Goal: 5–10 paying clients generating £2,000–5,000/mo recurring.

Stripe Payment Setup

Reuse the StakScan Stripe Account

No need to set up a new Stripe account. The existing StakScan account (live mode, UK sole trader) works fine. Just create new products and payment links inside the same dashboard. The statement descriptor will show as "STAK SCAN" on bank statements unless you update it — you might want to change it to "FASTGEO" or add a separate descriptor per product.

Products to create in Stripe:

GEO Starter — £150 one off payment

GEO Kickstart — £397 one off payment

Monthly GEO Package — £497/month recurring subscription

Premium Buildout — £997/month recurring subscription

How Payment Links Work

Stripe Payment Links are hosted checkout pages — no backend code needed. Create a payment link for each product in the Stripe dashboard, then paste those links into the CTA buttons on fastgeo.co.uk/pricing. The "Get Started" buttons already point to contact.html, but once Stripe links are live, you can swap them to go directly to checkout. For monthly plans, Stripe handles the recurring billing automatically.
